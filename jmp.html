Heatwaves and Health at Birth

Abstract and draft available upon request.

This paper examines how trimester-specific exposure to heatwaves (≥3 days with Tmax >35°C) affects birth outcomes in Spain, linking administrative birth records (2010–2014) to high-resolution temperature data. I study birth weight, gestational age, and cesarean delivery. Third-trimester exposure increases birth weight and gestational duration and raises C-section probability; results are robust to checks and alternative definitions.
